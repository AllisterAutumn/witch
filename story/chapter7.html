She ran, ragged dress flying around around her ankles, pressing her face against every glass storefront, barely held tears streaming down her face. She barely noticed the people wildly dodging her, biting back screams and snatching their children away. She found him walking out of a cafe, arm in arm with a woman. When the couple saw her, the boy stumbled back, and the woman stepped in front of him, as if shielding him. The girl froze, panting, her legs shaking.

“H-hi,” she said.

They stared at her.
