She took a step forward, and the woman held her hand out. “You shouldn’t be here,” the woman said. The girl stared at the boy. “Why- where…” “I left the bucket by the bush,” he said. “You shouldn’t be here,” the woman repeated. “I’ve been waiting for you,” was all the girl could pathetically say. The boy grimaced, and swallowed visibly. He gasped, and held his stomach. The woman instantly turned to him, forgetting completely about the girl. “It’s ok, it’s ok, let it out.” The girl cocked her head, a small smile wiggling its way into her mouth. He was doing doing an exact imitation of her father. “No,” she said. “No. Please. I love you. I’m sorry. Don't leave me alone.”
